WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

Most app lockers phone home. This one can't.

Every design choice serves one promise: what you lock stays your business.

No internet permission

The app doesn't request internet access in its manifest — anyone can verify it. It literally cannot report which apps you lock, to us or to anyone.

Your fingerprint stays your phone's

Unlocking is Android's own biometric prompt. The App Lockr only ever hears "yes" or "no" — nothing about your fingerprint is stored, by us or in the app.

Re-locks when the screen sleeps

Hand your phone over and the moment the screen turns off, every locked app is locked again. No timers to configure, no session left open.

One tap to pause

Protection is a switch, not a ceremony. Turn it off for a minute, back on after — your app choices are remembered exactly as you left them.

It can't see inside your apps

Usage access only tells the locker which app comes to the front — never what's on screen or what you type. With no internet permission, there'd be nowhere to send it anyway.

Speaks your language

English, Deutsch, Español, Français, Italiano, 日本語, 한국어, 中文 — each written natively for its audience, not machine-translated.

UNDER THE HOOD

Security you can explain to a friend

No jargon required — but the details are here for the people who want them.

No internet permission — and you can verify it

Android apps must declare their permissions, and The App Lockr never declares internet access — a fact anyone can check, not a promise in a policy. Our build fails automatically if that permission ever sneaks in through a dependency.

Your fingerprint never touches the app

The biometric check is Android's own system sheet. The app receives a pass or a fail — never fingerprint data, which stays inside your phone's secure hardware where it always was.

Usage access, used narrowly

It's read for one fact: which app just came to the front, so a locked one can be gated. Not what's inside, not for how long, not a history — and nothing is ever logged.

The lock screen is just a door

"Display over other apps" lets the lock screen step in front of a protected app the instant it opens. That is the only thing the overlay is used for — it never draws over anything else.

Screen off means locked again

Locked apps re-arm the moment the display sleeps, so an unlocked session can't outlive the hand you put the phone in.

The honest trade-off

An app locker guards your privacy from the people around you — it isn't full-disk encryption. Someone who knows your phone's own PIN could uninstall it. For everyone else, your apps are simply shut, and we'd rather tell you that plainly.

QUESTIONS

Straight answers

How does it lock other apps?
With Android's usage access, The App Lockr notices a protected app opening and immediately puts its lock screen in front of it. Your phone's own fingerprint or PIN opens the door.
Is my fingerprint stored somewhere?
No. The check is Android's own biometric prompt; the app is only told whether it passed. Nothing about your fingerprint exists in the app — and with no internet permission, it couldn't be sent anywhere anyway.
Can it see what I do inside my apps?
No. Usage access tells it which app came to the front — never screen content, keystrokes, or messages. It keeps no history, and it has no network access to send anything.
What happens when I hand someone my phone?
They can use everything you didn't lock. The moment the screen turns off, every locked app re-locks — so what's yours stays yours.
Is it really pay-once?
Yes — $4.99 one time on Google Play. No subscription, no in-app purchases, no ads.
